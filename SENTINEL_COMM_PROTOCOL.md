# SENTINEL 001 — Communications Protocol
**Effective:** 2026-04-30 | **Owner:** Program Coordinator | **Review:** Quarterly

---

## The Golden Rule

> **Email and Teams are for notifying. The portal is for recording.**

A decision, action, or commitment only exists officially once it is logged in the portal. If it is not in the portal, it did not happen for program purposes.

---

## Communication Channels — When to Use What

| Channel | Use For | Do NOT Use For |
|---|---|---|
| **Email** | Formal notifications, distributing documents, record requests | Making decisions, assigning actions |
| **Microsoft Teams** | Real-time coordination, quick clarifications, meeting logistics | Final decisions, binding commitments |
| **Portal — Comms Log** | Recording outcomes of all communications | Back-and-forth conversation |
| **Portal — Action Items** | All assigned work with owners and due dates | Informal to-dos |
| **Portal — TQ Log** | Technical queries to/from DNV | General technical discussion |
| **Text / Call** | Urgent time-sensitive notifications only | Any substantive discussion |

---

## What MUST Be Logged (and When)

Log to the portal **within 24 hours** of any communication that produces:

| If this happened... | Log it as... |
|---|---|
| A decision was made | Communication Log entry — Decisions field |
| An action was assigned to someone | Action Item — with owner and due date |
| A commitment was made by any party | Communication Log entry + Action Item |
| A document was reviewed verbally | Communication Log entry linked to the submission |
| A technical question was raised | TQ Log entry (or ECF if experience-related) |
| An executive was briefed | Communication Log entry — Executive lane |
| A contract interpretation was discussed | Communication Log entry — Contractual lane |
| A cost impact was identified | Communication Log entry — Cost lane |

**What does NOT need to be logged:**
- Scheduling logistics ("Can we move the call to 2pm?")
- Acknowledgments with no outcome ("Got it, thanks")
- Purely social exchanges

---

## Communication Lanes

Every logged communication and action item is tagged to one lane. Use the lane that reflects **who owns the outcome**, not just who was on the call.

### EXECUTIVE
- **Audience:** Program leadership, client senior management
- **Formality:** High — briefings, status reports, go/no-go decisions
- **Typical sources:** Executive reviews, milestone calls, program-level risk discussions
- **Owned by:** Program Manager / Lead Coordinator

### TECHNICAL
- **Audience:** Engineering teams, DNV reviewers, discipline leads
- **Formality:** Medium — technical queries, RFIs, design reviews, RID dispositions
- **Typical sources:** DNV comment responses, TQ exchanges, drawing reviews, ECF discussions
- **Owned by:** Responsible Engineer / Discipline Lead

### CONTRACTUAL
- **Audience:** Contracts team, DNV class team, legal
- **Formality:** High — formal correspondence, CDRL submittals, contract modifications, compliance decisions
- **Typical sources:** Formal letters, submittal cover sheets, contractual notifications
- **Owned by:** Contracts / Program Coordinator

### COST
- **Audience:** Project controls, finance, program manager
- **Formality:** Medium — budget decisions, SubPay approvals, cost impacts from technical changes
- **Typical sources:** Cost review calls, invoice discussions, budget change notifications
- **Owned by:** Project Controls / Program Manager

## Action Item Rules

1. **An action is not valid unless it exists in the portal.** Verbal agreements and Teams chat tasks do not count.
2. Every action must have an **owner** (one person, not a team) and a **due date**.
3. Actions discovered in external comms must be logged within **24 hours**.
4. Status must be updated in the portal when work progresses — not just when it closes.
5. Overdue actions (past due date) must be either closed, re-dated with justification, or escalated. They cannot simply age indefinitely.
6. If an action changes lane (e.g., a technical issue becomes an executive-level flag), update the lane and add a note explaining the escalation.

---

## Escalation Protocol

When an item needs to move to a higher-visibility lane:

1. Update the action's **Lane** field in the portal to the target lane.
2. Add a **note** explaining what triggered the escalation and when.
3. Notify the relevant lane owner directly (email or Teams) with the portal action ID in the subject line.
4. The original technical record remains — escalation is additive, not a replacement.

**Escalation triggers (minimum):**
- Technical issue with cost impact > [project threshold]
- Contractual interpretation that affects schedule
- Any item overdue by more than 5 business days without resolution
- Any item an engineer cannot resolve at their level

## SLA Targets

| Lane | Response Expectation |
|---|---|
| Executive | Acknowledged within 1 business day; resolved per program schedule |
| Technical | DNV TQ response: 10 business days (portal SLA tracked) |
| Contractual | Formal correspondence: 10 business days per contract terms |
| Cost | Cost impact notification: 5 business days from identification |

---

## Who Logs What

| Situation | Who Logs |
|---|---|
| You were on the call/email and a decision was made | You log it |
| Your team member had a conversation that produced an action for you | They log it, or you log it — whoever catches it first |
| A DNV TQ arrives | Coordinator logs it to TQ Log |
| An executive asks for a status update | The person who gave the update logs the outcome |
| A commitment is made in a Teams chat | Person who made the commitment logs it |

**Conflict rule:** If two people both log the same event, one entry is deleted and the other is kept. Duplicates are preferable to gaps.

---

## Quick Reference — How to Log

1. Open the portal → **Comms** tab
2. Click **+ Log Communication**
3. Fill in: Source, Lane, Date, Subject, Summary (2–3 sentences), Decisions (if any)
4. Link to a record if applicable (submission number, TQ ID, etc.)
5. Extract any action items inline — set owner and due date before saving
6. Click **Log Communication**

Total time: under 3 minutes per entry.
